<section class="grid-two">
  <div class="card">
    <h2>About the workshop</h2>
    <p>
      LIGHT focuses on the transition from large foundation models to compact,
      deployable AI systems that remain efficient, governable and suitable for
      industrial and regulated environments.
    </p>
    <p>
      The workshop brings together researchers working on distillation,
      compression, quantization, pruning, neuro-symbolic AI, verification,
      runtime guarantees and deployment under operational constraints.
    </p>
  </div>

  <div class="card">
    <h2>Core themes</h2>
    <ul>
      <li>Small language models and compact multimodal systems</li>
      <li>Trustworthiness-by-design through model compression</li>
      <li>Explainability, reasoning and verification</li>
      <li>Robustness, safety and runtime guarantees</li>
      <li>Edge and resource-constrained deployment</li>
      <li>Sustainable and energy-efficient AI</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="card">
  <h2>Why now?</h2>
  <p>
    AI systems are increasingly expected to run under cost, latency, privacy,
    sustainability and governance constraints. LIGHT aims to consolidate an
    emerging research community around compact AI systems that are not only
    accurate, but also operationally trustworthy and deployable.
  </p>
</section>
